Tool Selection and Design Overview
For this task, I used Canva to create a one-page infographic aimed at helping my batchmates navigate the digital world. The design focuses on three essential pillars: defining digital literacy, building a professional online presence, and mastering email etiquette. I chose a clean, professional layout with specific icons—like a brain connected to Wi-Fi and a graduation cap—to make the information visually engaging and easy to follow.

The first section explains that digital literacy is about more than just typing; it is the ability to find and evaluate information. The second section reminds students that their "Digital Footprint" is their new resume, encouraging them to curate professional skills on platforms like LinkedIn. Finally, the email etiquette section provides a simple checklist for clear subject lines and professional salutations, such as "Dear Professor."

Reflections on the Process
One thing I found particularly interesting was the challenge of "information shedding." It was difficult to condense broad topics into just a few bullet points. However, I realized that for an infographic to be effective, it must be direct. Distilling professional presence into a simple tip like "Google yourself" was a great exercise in prioritizing the most impactful advice for students.

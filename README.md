# DIGITAL-LITERACY
# TASK 1
Tool Selection and Design Overview
For this task, I used Canva to design a high-impact, one-page visual resource titled "Digital Literacy: Your Key to the Digital World." The design is segmented into three distinct, color-coded sections to ensure information is digestible at a glance. I utilized a vibrant palette incorporating teal, amber, and sage green—to create a professional yet welcoming aesthetic suitable for a student audience.

The infographic highlights three critical domains: a clear definition of Digital Literacy, a showcase of Essential Digital Tools and a checklist for Online Safety. By pairing industry-standard icons with concise bullet points, the design emphasizes practical utility over dense theory, making it an effective quick-reference guide for my batchmates.

Reflections on the Process
One aspect I found particularly interesting was the challenge of visual hierarchy. I had to carefully balance the size of the "Essential Tools" logos against the safety tips to ensure one section didn't overwhelm the other. Distilling "Stay Safe Online" into just three actionable phrases—strong passwords, critical thinking, and source verification—was difficult but rewarding, as it forced me to prioritize the most vital information for immediate student impact.
